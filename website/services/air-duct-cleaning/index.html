Our Professional Cleaning Process

We follow a comprehensive 8-step process to ensure thorough cleaning and maximum results

1

Initial Inspection

We thoroughly inspect your HVAC system, ductwork, and air vents to assess contamination levels and identify any issues.

2

System Access Setup

We create access points in your ductwork and connect our powerful truck-mounted vacuum system.

3

Protective Preparation

We protect your home with drop cloths and seal off work areas to prevent dust and debris from spreading.

4

Agitation & Loosening

Using specialized brushes and air tools, we agitate and loosen contaminants from duct walls and surfaces.

5

Powerful Vacuum Extraction

Our truck-mounted vacuum system creates negative pressure to extract all loosened debris and contaminants.

6

Vent & Register Cleaning

We remove and thoroughly clean all air vents, registers, and grilles before reinstalling them.

7

Sanitization (Optional)

We can apply EPA-approved antimicrobial treatment to eliminate bacteria, mold, and other microorganisms.

8

Final Inspection & Cleanup

We perform a final inspection, provide before/after photos, and clean up our work area completely.

Signs Your Air Ducts Need Cleaning

Watch for these warning signs that indicate it's time for professional air duct cleaning

Visible Signs

  • Dust buildup around air vents and registers
  • Visible mold growth in or around HVAC components
  • Excessive dust on furniture despite regular cleaning
  • Dark or dirty marks around vent openings
  • Debris or particles blowing out of vents
  • Clogged air filters that need frequent replacement

Performance Issues

  • Reduced airflow from vents
  • Inconsistent temperatures between rooms
  • Higher than normal energy bills
  • HVAC system running longer than usual
  • Strange noises from ductwork
  • Frequent HVAC repairs needed

Health & Comfort Indicators

  • Worsening allergies or asthma symptoms
  • Persistent musty or stale odors
  • Increased dust allergies indoors
  • Family members getting sick more often
  • Respiratory irritation or coughing
  • Pet odors lingering despite cleaning

Circumstantial Factors

  • Recent home renovation or construction
  • New pets in the home
  • After wildfire season exposure
  • Moving into a previously occupied home
  • It's been 3+ years since last cleaning
  • Previous water damage or flooding
